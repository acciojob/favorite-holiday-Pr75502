Holi


Holi is a popular Hindu festival, also known as the Festival of Colors. It marks the arrival of spring and the victory of good over evil. Celebrated with great enthusiasm, people throw colored powders, splash water, and enjoy festive sweets like gujiya. The festival begins with Holika Dahan, symbolizing the burning of negativity, followed by a day of joy, music, and dance.

  1. Mathura & Vrindavan – Experience the divine **Phoolon Ki Holi** at the Banke Bihari Temple.
  2. Barsana & Nandgaon – Witness the unique Lathmar Holi, where women playfully hit men with sticks.
  3. Jaipur, Rajasthan – Enjoy the grand Elephant Festival featuring decorated elephants and folk performances.
  4. Shantiniketan, West Bengal – Celebrate the cultural Basanta Utsav, inspired by Rabindranath Tagore.
  5. Udaipur, Rajasthan – Witness the royal Holika Dahan and the majestic City Palace Holi.
  • Gujiya – A sweet dumpling filled with khoya, dry fruits, and sugar.
  • Thandai – A refreshing drink made with milk, saffron, dry fruits, and spices.
  • Malpua – A crispy and soft Indian pancake soaked in sugar syrup.
  • Dahi Bhalla – Soft lentil dumplings soaked in curd and topped with tangy chutneys.
  • Kanji – A fermented drink made with mustard seeds, black carrots, and Indian spices.
